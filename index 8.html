About Alby

Welcome to the Greenwood

I've always believed that the best stories find you when you least expect them — usually hiding under a leaf, or curled inside a particularly interesting creature.

For three decades I built a career helping families plan their futures — but all the while, I was scribbling poems on notepads and spinning tales for my two boys that were definitely more entertaining than they needed to be.

Inspired by the wild beauty of Tasmania, a lifelong love of rhyme, and a childhood spent with Dr Seuss, I write picture books that celebrate the small, overlooked wonders of the natural world.

I live on the edge of a great adventure — quite literally — and I believe every child deserves a story that makes them look twice at the world around them.

The Most Marvellous Beetle

Jimmy knows everything about beetles. Everything. His teacher, his family, and just about everyone at school knows this too — whether they want to or not. But when Jimmy spots something extraordinary on a bush in the school yard — a Miena Jewel Beetle, one of the rarest creatures on the planet — suddenly the boy everyone tried to escape becomes the hero nobody expected. A joyful, funny picture book about the superpower of knowing one thing really, really well, and the magnificent moment the world finally catches up with you.

Picture Book · Ages 3–7 · EK Books "Your Marvellous Mind" Competition
02
Manuscript · In Discussion

The Rock Face

A boy arrives in a new country carrying nothing but a backpack and a longing to belong. When he discovers rock climbing, he finds more than just a sport — he finds a language that everyone speaks, and a place where he finally feels at home.

Picture Book · Ages 4–8 · Themes of belonging & resilience
